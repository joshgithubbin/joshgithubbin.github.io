I graduated from The University of Southampton in 2020 with First Class Honours. It was an absolutely fantastic chapter of my life and I loved every minute! Work-wise my modules ranged from cosmological theory, to practical lab and programming work, to abstract mathematics of quantum physics and beyond!
My grade average at Southampton steadily increased year after year. In my second year my grades were high enough for me to be selected for a module in Tenerife, which involved both a telescope design component with students from other universities and a self-led observation project at the Observatorio del Teide!
My Masters project involved dust reverberation mapping in Type I AGN; a project involving a hefty amount of research, teamwork, and Python programming. It was incredibly fun and, requiring a combination of linear regression and ensemble sampling, set me off on the path of self-study regarding machine learning techniques.
My primary interests in astronomy involve galaxies – AGN, star formation regions, and evolution, having studied these topics for my Masters, Tenerife project, and third-year dissertation respectively. I also really enjoyed the cosmology modules taken in my final year, which combined mathematics and astronomical theory.
