Currently I work as a risk analyst for Nationwide, where I monitor and develop scorecards used in customer product applications such as credit cards and current accounts. The job involves a lot of data analysis techniques (including machine learning) and communicating with my peers in various ways including papers and presentations.
In my spare time I have been working on a series of literature reviews regarding the history of pre-telescopic astronomy to gain a better understanding of humanity's interest in the sky. Currently I'm editing the first review, which examines evidence of basic astronomical advancement in ancient Afro-Eurasia.
I still revisit physics theory when I find the time - I like revisiting the modules I found particularly challenging to make sure the knowledge I developed remains sharp. I develop skills in lots of other ways through my hobbies; taking a look on my GitHub should give you a small idea of the other stuff I get up to on slow days!
