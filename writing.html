My Masters project required two pieces of written work; a paper for experts, detailing every aspect of the project; and a short two-page summary for those with no experience in astronomy. Both were received positively by their respective audiences and were awarded a high grade.
My third-year dissertation was a literature review detailing the evolution of galaxies. While I am currently working on an updated edition to better reflect my current knowledge of the topic it holds a special place in my heart for being the piece of work that really convinced me of my love of galaxies.
My latest piece regarding physics (and last piece of work I submitted for my degree) examines the solar wind and its influence on the magnetospheres of the Earth and Moon. While I hadn’t studied space plasma physics before this module I found it an incredibly interesting topic!
Fiction writing was always something I wanted to go into when I was younger, and the time I spend on it as a casual hobby thankfully makes writing reports and literature reviews something I find pretty fun! For the past several years I’ve taken part in NaNoWriMo, which encourages you to write 50,000 words in a single month.
